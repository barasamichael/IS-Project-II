Accommodation

SettleBot provides personalized accommodation guidance based on your specific needs:

  • Recommends areas near your university with price ranges
  • Explains the rental process and required documents
  • Provides safety ratings for different neighborhoods
  • Helps you understand typical costs and what's included
  • Guides you through the application and inspection process
  • Advises on negotiation strategies and red flags to avoid

Just tell SettleBot your budget, preferred location, and requirements, and get tailored recommendations.

SettleBot can guide you through the complete documentation requirements, which typically include:

  • Passport copy and student visa
  • Student ID and admission letter
  • Bank statements or financial proof
  • Guarantor information (sometimes required)
  • Passport-sized photos
  • Advance rent payment (usually 2-3 months)

Requirements can vary by landlord and property type, so SettleBot provides specific guidance based on your situation and chosen accommodation type.

SettleBot provides current, location-specific pricing information. Generally, student accommodation ranges:

  • University hostels: KSh 12,000-18,000/month
  • Shared apartments: KSh 15,000-25,000/month
  • Studio apartments: KSh 20,000-35,000/month
  • One-bedroom apartments: KSh 25,000-45,000/month

Costs vary significantly by area, with locations closer to universities typically being more expensive. SettleBot can provide specific price ranges based on your chosen university and preferences.

SettleBot teaches you how to verify legitimate accommodation options and avoid scams:

  • Always visit the property in person before paying
  • Verify the landlord's ownership documents
  • Check if the property has proper water and electricity connections
  • Ask for references from previous tenants
  • Ensure rent agreements are properly documented
  • Be cautious of deals that seem too good to be true

SettleBot can guide you through red flags to watch for and provide a checklist for property viewing.

SettleBot helps you weigh the pros and cons based on your priorities:

University accommodation advantages:

  • Guaranteed safe and verified housing
  • Easier application process for international students

  • Close proximity to campus facilities
  • Usually includes utilities and internet

Private housing advantages:

  • More privacy and independence

SettleBot can help you evaluate your specific needs and make the best choice for your situation.

Utility inclusion varies significantly between properties. SettleBot helps you understand what to expect:

Commonly included:

  • Water (most properties include this)
  • Security services
  • Garbage collection

Often separate costs:

  • Electricity (usually prepaid meters)
  • Internet and Wi-Fi
  • Gas (if cooking with gas)
  • Parking fees

Always clarify utility arrangements before signing a lease. SettleBot provides guidance on typical costs for each utility type.

Administrative Processes

SettleBot guides you through the complete bank account opening process:

Recommended banks for students: KCB, Equity Bank, Cooperative Bank, and Barclays all offer student-friendly accounts.

Required documents:

  • Passport with valid visa
  • Student ID and admission letter
  • Proof of residence (rental agreement or hostel letter)
  • Passport-sized photos
  • Minimum deposit (usually KSh 1,000-5,000)

The process typically takes 1-2 business days. SettleBot can recommend which bank branches are most convenient to your location and explain the different account types available.

Yes, international students in Kenya need a student pass (permit). SettleBot provides step-by-step guidance:

Application process:

  • Apply online through the eCitizen portal
  • Submit required documents and pay fees
  • Schedule biometric appointment
  • Attend appointment at immigration offices
  • Collect your permit once approved

Required documents:

  • Completed application form
  • Passport-sized photos
  • Admission letter from recognized institution
  • Medical certificate
  • Financial proof (bank statements)
  • Police clearance from home country

Processing typically takes 2-4 weeks. SettleBot helps you prepare all documents correctly to avoid delays.

Getting a Kenyan mobile number is straightforward, and SettleBot guides you through the options:

Major networks: Safaricom, Airtel Kenya, and Telkom Kenya all serve international students.

Registration requirements:

  • Valid passport
  • Passport-sized photo
  • Proof of residence (rental agreement or hostel letter)

Process:

  • Visit any authorized dealer or network shop
  • Choose your preferred package (prepaid recommended for students)
  • Complete registration form
  • SIM activation usually takes 24-48 hours

SettleBot can help you compare data packages and calling rates to choose the best option for your needs.

SettleBot explains the various healthcare options for international students:

University health insurance: Many universities offer group health insurance policies for international students. Check with your institution first.

Private insurance options:

  • AAR Healthcare (comprehensive coverage)
  • Resolution Health (student-friendly packages)
  • Britam Health (affordable basic coverage)
  • Madison Health (good emergency coverage)

National Hospital Insurance Fund (NHIF): You may be eligible to join NHIF for basic coverage once you have a student permit.

SettleBot helps you understand coverage options, costs, and application processes for each insurance type.

SettleBot explains various money transfer options with their pros and cons:

International services:

  • Western Union (widely available, higher fees)
  • MoneyGram (good coverage, moderate fees)
  • Wise (formerly TransferWise) - competitive exchange rates
  • Remitly (online transfers, good rates)

Bank transfers:

  • SWIFT transfers (secure but expensive)
  • Correspondent bank relationships
  • Online banking international transfers

Digital options:

  • M-Pesa international (from select countries)
  • PayPal (limited but growing in Kenya)

SettleBot helps you compare fees, exchange rates, and transfer times to choose the best option for your needs.

SettleBot provides comprehensive transportation guidance for getting around Nairobi:

Public transportation:

  • Matatus: Shared minibuses (KSh 20-100 per ride, most common)
  • Buses: City Hoppa, Double M (KSh 30-80, more comfortable)
  • Train: Nairobi Commuter Rail (limited routes, KSh 20-50)

Ride-hailing services:

  • Uber (widely available, cashless)
  • Bolt (competitive pricing)
  • Little Cab (local alternative)

Other options:

  • Bodabodas (motorcycle taxis) for short distances
  • Walking (many university areas are walkable)
  • Bicycle (growing infrastructure in some areas)

SettleBot helps you plan routes and understand safety considerations for each transportation type.

University registration processes vary, but SettleBot guides you through common requirements:

Pre-arrival:

  • Complete online pre-registration if available
  • Submit all required academic documents
  • Pay initial fees or deposits
  • Apply for accommodation if needed

Upon arrival:

  • Visit the International Students Office first

  • Submit original documents for verification

  • Attend orientation sessions

Common documents needed:

  • Original academic transcripts and certificates
  • Passport and student visa
  • Medical examination results
  • Proof of fee payment

SettleBot provides university-specific guidance based on where you'll be studying.
